About Me

I am a junior at Rutgers University (NB) majoring in astrophysics and mathematics. At Rutgers, I am a member of the Society of Physics Students (in which I am a member of the computing cluster admin), the Rutgers Undergraduate Mathematics Association (RUMA), and the Rutgers Astronomical Society (RAS). Sometimes, I participate in the Rutgers Undergraduate-Graduate Mathematical Physics Seminar.

Research/Research Interests

Currently, I am conducting gravitational lensing research under Professor Charles Keeton in the Department of Physics and Astronomy. Very briefly, our research seeks to quantify the effect of cosmic weak lensing on the lens equation using ray-tracing algorithms. The results from this project will help in creating better lens models for cluster lensing science.
Outside of astrophysics, I am currently taking an independent study course under Professor Filip Dul and Professor A. Shadi Tahvildar-Zadeh in the Department of Mathematics. In this course, we will be working through a broad set of mathematical tools used in quantum field theory (QFT) such as: (1) abstract algebra, (2) functional analysis and PDE, and (3) geometry.
In the future, I would like to pursue theoretical/mathematical physics. In particular, I am attracted to relativity/gravitation/quantum field theory - especially how they apply to astrophysics/cosmology. For instance, my main research interest would be the mathematics behind black hole formation.
