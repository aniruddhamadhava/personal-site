Research/Research Interests

Currently, I am conducting gravitational lensing research under Professor Charles Keeton in the Department of Physics and Astronomy. Gravitational lensing has become a powerful astrophysical tool, acting as cosmic telescopes to the high-redshift universe. In particular, with masses of $\sim 10^{14} - 10^{15} \hspace{2pt} M_{\odot}$, galaxy clusters offer some of the strongest lenses of high-redshift galaxies. Before these images can be reconstructed to study the source, it is important to model the cluster lens. Previous work have considered different parameters for this lens modeling process, but have not included effects of large-scale cosmological structures (LSS). This work specifically considers the effects of LSS density fluctuations on differential deflection fields on cluster lens modeling.
Outside of astrophysics, I am currently taking an independent study course under Professor Filip Dul and Professor A. Shadi Tahvildar-Zadeh in the Department of Mathematics. In this course, we will be working through a broad set of mathematical tools used in quantum field theory (QFT) such as: (1) abstract algebra, (2) functional analysis and PDE, and (3) geometry.
In the future, I would like to pursue theoretical/mathematical physics. In particular, I am attracted to relativity/gravitation/quantum field theory - especially how they apply to astrophysics/cosmology. For instance, my main research interest would be the mathematics behind black hole formation.
